The Paradox Explained

The Monty Hall problem is famous because it creates a conflict between your intuition and actual math.

1. The Setup (The Game Show)

Imagine you are on a game show. There are 3 closed doors in front of you:

  • Behind one door is a brand new Car üöó.
  • Behind the other two are Goats üêê.

2. The Rules

  • You Pick: You point to a door (e.g., Door 1).
  • Monty Reveals: The host (who knows the truth) opens one of the other doors to show a Goat.
  • Crucial Rule: Monty will never open your door, and he will never open the Car door.
  • The Choice: He asks: "Do you want to switch or stay?"

3. The "Problem"

Your Brain Says: "Two doors left. One car, one goat. It's a 50/50 coin flip. Why switch?"

The Reality: It is NOT 50/50. The game is rigged so the "Other Door" is twice as likely to have the car.

4. Why isn't it 50/50?

Monty didn't pick randomly. He filtered the choices.

  • Your Door (1/3 chance): This represents your guess when you knew nothing.
  • The "Other" Doors (2/3 chance): Monty eliminated the bad option from this group. The entire 2/3 chance concentrates on the one remaining door.
