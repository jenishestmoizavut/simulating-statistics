The Magic of Pi

In the 18th century, Georges-Louis Leclerc, Comte de Buffon, asked a question: "If I drop a needle on a floor made of parallel strips of wood, what is the chance the needle crosses a line?"

The green lines are 'needles' which intersect on the parallel strips of wood. The brown ones are those which perfectly fit in.

If the needle length equals the width of the strips, the probability turns out to be exactly:

P = 2 / π

This means we can calculate Pi by just counting drops!

π ≈ 2 × (Total / Crosses)
