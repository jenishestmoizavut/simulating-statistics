The Magic of π

In the 18th century, the Comte de Buffon discovered that if you drop needles on parallel lines, the probability of crossing a line is related to π.

If the needle length ($l$) equals the line spacing ($d$):

P = 2 / π

π ≈ 2 × (Total / Crosses)
